Welcome to Asha Kiran

Founded in 2005, Asha Kiran is a sanctuary for abandoned children and elderly individuals who have nowhere else to turn. What began as a small shelter with just 5 children has blossomed into a loving home for 82 residents.

Located in the outskirts of Bangalore, our 5-acre campus provides a nurturing environment where forgotten members of society find family, care and dignity.

Mrs. Lakshmi Reddy

Founder & Director

The Heart Behind Our Home

Mrs. Lakshmi Reddy's vision for Asha Kiran was born from personal tragedy. After losing her own parents at a young age and later discovering abandoned elderly individuals during her nursing career, she felt called to create a bridge between generations that needed each other.

In 2005, she sold her nursing home and used her life savings to purchase land and build the first shelter. What began with just 5 orphans and 2 elderly residents has grown exponentially through community support.

"These children and elders healed each other in ways I never imagined. The loneliness disappeared when they became family."

Our Journey

2005 - Humble Beginnings

Opened with just 5 children and 2 elderly residents in a rented house. Mrs. Reddy mortgaged her jewelry to cover the first year's expenses.

2010 - Permanent Home

Purchased land and built our current campus through generous donations. Expanded capacity to house 50 residents.

2015 - A Decade of Hope

Celebrated our 10th anniversary with 65 residents. Added vocational training programs for older children.

Today - Thriving Community

Now home to 52 children and 30 elderly residents. Expanded facilities include classrooms, medical center, and organic farm.

Our Mission & Vision

Mission

To provide abandoned children and elderly individuals with a loving home environment that nurtures their physical, emotional, and spiritual needs while preserving dignity and fostering independence.

Vision

To expand our capacity to serve 100+ residents by 2025 while developing vocational training programs that prepare our youth for success and provide meaningful activities for our elders.
